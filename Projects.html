Neural Spike Sorting and Analysis using Principal Component Analysis

January 2024 – February 2024

This project delved into the fascinating realm of neural data analysis, employing MATLAB to unlock insights from recorded neural activity. The objective was to identify and distinguish individual action potentials (spikes) from background noise, providing a deeper understanding of neuronal communication. Through the implementation of Principal Component Analysis (PCA), the project successfully clustered and visualized the extracted spikes, revealing hidden patterns and potential insights into neuronal function.

Development of Iron-Based Nanoparticles for Hyperthermia

January 2022 – April 2022

The journey into nanomaterial synthesis unfolded as I embarked on the development of Iron-Based Nanoparticles (IONP) for hyperthermia. With precision, I synthesized IONPs with a yield size of 11nm through a co-precipitation method, showcasing proficiency in nanomaterial synthesis. My innovation unfolded as I pioneered a novel approach, developing IONP conjugated with HA gel, achieving a 13nm particle size. Experimental triumphs followed, with the magnetic hyperthermia potential of HA-IONPs reaching temperatures of 48°C under RF exposure, inducing a remarkable 26% greater cytotoxicity compared to controls. Practical contributions extended to testing HA-IONPs in a tissue-mimicking phantom, evaluating the efficacy of hyperthermia applications and making strides in medical research.

Finite Element Analysis of Femur Bone

March – May 2021

Diving into the structural intricacies of the human body, I embarked on a Finite Element Analysis of the femur bone. Armed with DICOM files obtained from a CT scan, I meticulously created a three-dimensional model. Importing this model into ANSYS, a finite element code, I conducted stress analysis, unveiling insights into areas of high-stress concentration. This endeavor encapsulated my dedication to understanding and analyzing the mechanical intricacies of biological structures.

Research Proposals

Designing a Wearable for Continuous Stress Monitoring

November – December 2023

I devised a conceptual wearable that provides continuous, accurate stress monitoring by integrating data from multiple sensors. It contains sensors to measure cortisol, heart rate variability, skin temperature, and activity levels. Raw sensor data is processed through a pipeline to extract relevant features. Personalized machine learning models classify real-time stress levels based on these features.

My focus was building an optimized system that provides accurate stress insights through intuitive visualizations, without overwhelming users with technical data. This project highlights my expertise in wearable sensors, machine learning pipelines, health data visualization, and human-centered design of AI systems for digital health.

3D-Printed Gold Nanoparticle (AuNP) for Localized Surface Plasmon Resonance (LSPR) Based Detection for Biosensing Application

October – December 2023

I aim to create novel biosensors using 3D printed gold nanoparticles and surface plasmon resonance technology. This allows low-cost, eco-friendly fabrication tailored for ultrasensitive biomolecule detection. I will optimize 3D printing to generate uniform gold nanoparticle thin films and functionalize them for selective biosensing. By developing optical instrumentation to detect varying DNA concentrations, I will demonstrate proof-of-concept for this reliable detection platform. This project highlights my expertise in nanofabrication, bioconjugation, optical sensing, and leveraging 3D printing to solve complex engineering challenges. It could enable rapid manufacturing of affordable point-of-care diagnostic devices.
