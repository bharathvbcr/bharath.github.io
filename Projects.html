Development of Iron-Based Nanoparticles for Hyperthermia

January 2022 – April 2022

My fascination with nanomaterials led me to a 4-month project exploring their potential in hyperthermia, a heat-based cancer treatment. I honed my skills by synthesizing 11nm iron oxide nanoparticles (IONPs).

But I craved more. I wanted uniform heating and biocompatibility, so I pioneered a novel approach: HA-IONPs, combining IONPs with biocompatible hydroxyapatite (HA) gel. These 13nm particles excelled, reaching 48°C under radiofrequency exposure and inducing 26% greater cell death than controls.

To bridge the gap to real-world applications, I tested HA-IONPs in a tissue-mimicking phantom, simulating their effectiveness within human tissue.

This project not only solidified my skills in nanomaterial development and innovation but also unveiled the exciting potential of nanomedicine in the fight against cancer.

Conversion of Compound Microscope into a Fluorescence Microscope for Bioimaging

July 2022 – December 2021

Fueled by a desire to make advanced tools accessible, I engineered and validated a cost-effective microscopy system delivering high-end image quality at just 10% of the typical cost. This innovative system opens doors for affordable, high-performance imaging solutions.

I led the validation process, using the system to image DAPI-stained endothelial cells under UV light and benchmarking the results against a high-end microscope. The images confirmed the system's remarkable capability and accuracy.

Beyond cost savings, the system's swift image acquisition and streamlined analysis improve diagnostic capabilities, empowering researchers and clinicians to diagnose and treat diseases with greater efficiency and accuracy.

This project highlights my ability to develop innovative, accessible healthcare technology.

Finite Element Analysis of Femur Bone

March – May 2021

My curiosity about bone mechanics led me to build a 3D model of a femur and analyze its stress distribution using ANSYS software. This project honed my ability to leverage technology for biomechanical research, pinpointing high-stress areas relevant to fields like implant design, orthopedics, and sports medicine.
