Research Assistant

Jul 2021 - May 2022

Advanced Nano-Theranostics (ANTs) Research Group, SRM IST

Chennai, India

Nestled in the bustling city of Chennai, India, my academic journey reached new heights with the ANTs Research Group at SRM IST. Leading a cross-functional team of four, we delved into the world of cancer research, utilizing iron-based nanoparticles to generate localized heat for hyperthermia treatment. Our collective efforts were showcased at the Dr. Paarivendhar Research Colloquium, where I presented our groundbreaking research on the "Feasibility of radiofrequency hyperthermia using iron oxide hydrogel for prostate cancer treatment."

Collaboration took center stage as we worked on a project to design microfluidic chips aimed at isolating circulating tumor cells. I found myself contributing expertise to the progression of innovative technologies in cancer research, each experiment and breakthrough a step closer to making a tangible impact.

Adding an experimental flair to my repertoire, I crafted a tissue-mimicking phantom suitable for in-vivo studies, demonstrating a proficiency in design and execution that echoed in the laboratory corridors.

The pinnacle of my academic journey came as I re-engineered a bright-field microscope into a fluorescence microscope, a transformation that not only enhanced bio-imaging capabilities but also symbolized the adaptability and ingenuity that defined my academic endeavors.
